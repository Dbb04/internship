Bhagat Singh

Birth: September 28, 1907

Death: March 23, 1931

Known for: His role in the Indian independence movement

About Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh was a charismatic Indian revolutionary whose two acts of dramatic violence against the British in India and execution at age 23 made him a folk hero of the Indian independence movement.

He was born in a Sikh family in Punjab, British India, in 1907. His family had earlier been involved in revolutionary activities against the British Raj.

He was deeply influenced by the freedom fighters of his time and joined the Hindustan Republican Association, which was later renamed the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association.

Bhagat Singh is remembered as Shaheed (Martyr) Bhagat Singh, and his legacy continues to inspire many in India and around the world.
